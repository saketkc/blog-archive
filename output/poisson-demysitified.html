Poisson Demysitified

Learnt it the hard way. This one is more of a scratch pad, inspired from the previously discussed problme on estimating the size of restriction fragments.

Notations:

\(N_t\)= Number of events that have happened in the \(t^{th}\) time interval

\(T\) = Time taken till the first arrival occurs(or the next arrival occurs since waiting time is memory less i.e$)i

Now consider the event, \(\{T > x \}\) so you had to wait \(x\) seconds before your first bus came and the buses wre coming at a rate of \(\lambda\) per second.

\(\{T > x\} \sim N_1 = 0\)
